Experiments

Experimental Setup

All agents interact with the same stochastic bakery simulator described in Section 2. Episodes last 240 ticks (≈40 minutes), with an average of 60 customer arrivals per episode. Two ovens (capacity 4u each) manage baking operations, the queue caps at 12 customers, and customer patience is uniformly distributed between 30–90 seconds. Non-stationarity is enabled: demand distributions drift by ±10% day-to-day and item preferences shift by ±10% week-to-week.

Training protocols

  • PPO (v3 guardrails): trained for 1e6 steps, after an initial 3e5-step sanity pass.
  • PPO+Price Bandit: same PPO training budget, with either (i) composite penalty-based metric or (ii) profit-only metric.
  • PPO+Par: trained for 1e6 steps using a fixed par-level heuristic for baking.
  • Evaluation procedure:
    • Full horizon: 10 seeds × 20 evaluation days (per-seed daily means).
    • Weekly stability: 20 seeds × 5 days (per-seed 5-day means).
      These match standard RL evaluation practice by averaging both across seeds and across multiple episodes per seed.

Evaluation Metrics

We report both business KPIs and RL-internal reward components:

  • Net profit: revenue − ingredient cost − end-of-day leftover cost.
  • Abandonment rate: fraction of customers whose patience expired.
  • Average wait time: mean seconds waited by served customers.
  • Waste rate: fraction of baked items that expire unsold.

These KPIs reflect real operational goals: high throughput, low abandonment, and manageable waste.

RL agents, however, optimize the reward function, not the KPIs. The reward combines profit with explicit penalties for delay, abandonment, balking, and leftover inventory. This distinction becomes central in interpreting results.

Agent Comparisons

We evaluate the following policies:

  • Newsvendor baseline: strongest heuristic; computes per-day production targets from expected demand.
  • PPO (v3): deep policy-gradient agent with action masking.
  • PPO+Price (composite metric): Thompson Sampling over price multipliers using the RL reward for feedback.
  • PPO+Par: PPO paired with a simple par-level baking heuristic.
  • PPO+Price (profit-only): ablation isolating whether the bandit should optimize raw profit.
  • (If applicable: QRDQN results will later be inserted in Section 4.)

The consolidated results appear in the Results section of the site, including full-horizon and weekly comparisons. In brief:

  • Newsvendor achieves the highest profit and service quality.
  • PPO reduces waste significantly but at the cost of high abandonment and long waits.
  • PPO+Price does not outperform PPO unless the pricing feedback metric is strictly profit-based.
  • PPO+Par performs poorly because the par-level logic amplifies underproduction and chronic shortages.

Interpretation: these patterns follow directly from the structure of the reward function, not from instability or poor training.

Reward definition (formal)

[ R_t = _t - _w,_t - _a,_t - _b,_t - _L,_t + _s,_t, ] with
(_w = 0.01),
(_a = 0.7),
(_b = 0.1),
(_L = 1.0),
(_s = 0.1).

Consequence: PPO learns “safe but conservative” behavior

Because the cost of leftover inventory is heavily penalized and wait penalties accumulate every tick:

  • The agent strongly avoids baking unless queue pressure demands it.
  • This leads to chronic underproduction, long waits, and high abandonment.
  • Waste is minimized, but profit collapses relative to Newsvendor.

This misalignment, rather than algorithmic failure, fully explains the empirical results.
